# Phase 7 — Content Engine

The static pages built in Phases 2 + 3 are the "always-on" backbone. This phase keeps the site evergreen and earns the kind of inbound links and topical authority that move non-brand queries off the bench.

## 7.1 What's already shipped

Generated by `node build-pages.js` from [build/site-data.js](../build/site-data.js) + [build/templates.js](../build/templates.js):

- 5 location pages (one per yard, with `LocalBusiness` schema)
- 9 fleet tonnage pages (with `Service` + `Product` schema)
- 8 service pages (with `Service` schema)
- 5 industry hub pages (with `WebPage` + `BreadcrumbList` schema)
- 4 educational `/learn/` long-tail pages (with `Article` schema)

Every page cross-links: yards link to fleet, fleet links to yards and services, services link to fleet, industries link to fleet + services + yards. This is the internal link structure that signals topical authority to Google.

## 7.3 Case study template

Each case study lives at `/case-studies/{yard-slug}/{project-slug}/index.html`. Add a new section to [build/site-data.js](../build/site-data.js) (`CASE_STUDIES`) and a `renderCaseStudyPage` function in [build/templates.js](../build/templates.js); structure:

```js
{
  slug: 'baytown-coker-drum-exchange-2026',
  yard: 'baytown',
  customerType: 'Refinery operator',
  industry: 'refining-petrochem',
  cranesUsed: ['500-ton', '300-ton'],
  service: 'refinery-turnaround',
  city: 'Baytown',
  state: 'TX',
  publishedDate: '2026-06-04',
  headline: 'Coker drum exchange during a 28-day turnaround at a Baytown refinery',
  summary: '1-2 sentence editorial summary for cards and meta description.',
  challenge: '...',
  approach: '...',
  outcome: '...',
  liftSpecs: { weight: '350,000 lb', radius: '95 ft', height: '...' },
  customerQuote: { body: '...', attribution: 'Plant turnaround manager' },
  imagePaths: ['/img/case-studies/.../1.jpg', '...'],
}
```

The template should emit schema for `Article` + `Place` (the city) + reference back to the `Service` and fleet `Product` records.

### Length and structure
- 800–1,200 words.
- Hero photo, lift parameters table, customer quote, related links (yard, fleet class, service, industry).
- One internal link per major referenced entity (don't over-stuff).
- Photo captions count; write them with city + crane class + industry naturally.

## 7.5 Republish & refresh

Every quarter, pick the 4 oldest content pages (case study or `/learn/` article) and:
- Update copy where outdated.
- Re-shoot or swap stale photos.
- Add 2–3 new internal links to recently shipped pages.
- Update `datePublished` → `dateModified` in schema.
- Re-run `node build-pages.js` and the sitemap will reflect the new `lastmod`.

Google rewards demonstrably-fresh content. A monthly refresh ritual beats writing six fresh articles a year and forgetting them.

## 7.6 Topic clusters & internal linking

Internal linking from the `/learn/` articles is where most authority gets passed back to the money pages.

Rule: each `/learn/` article must include at least:
- 1 link to a relevant `/fleet/{slug}/` page.
- 1 link to a relevant `/services/{slug}/` page.
- 1 link to a relevant `/locations/{slug}/` page (or homepage if generic).
- 1 link to another `/learn/` article (the "related guides" block at the bottom does this automatically — keep it).

Each new case study must link back to the matching:
- Yard page (`/locations/{yard}/`)
- Fleet class(es) used (`/fleet/{slug}/`)
- Service used (`/services/{slug}/`)
- Industry (`/industries/{slug}/`)
